================================================================================
1. SCOPE AND PURPOSE
================================================================================

This manual defines ACME Corporation's data security and compliance
requirements. It applies to all information classified as Confidential,
Internal, or Public that is created, stored, processed, or transmitted
on company systems, as well as to any personal devices used for work.

ACME is subject to the following regulatory regimes:
- GDPR (EU/UK data protection)
- CCPA (California Consumer Privacy Act)
- SOC 2 Type II (annual audit)
- HIPAA (for protected health information in our Health division)
- ISO/IEC 27001:2022

================================================================================
2. DATA CLASSIFICATION
================================================================================

2.1 PUBLIC
Information explicitly approved for external distribution (e.g.,
marketing materials, published research). No special handling required.

2.2 INTERNAL
Default classification for most business information. Sharing outside
the company requires manager approval.

2.3 CONFIDENTIAL
Includes customer data, financial records, source code, security
architecture, employee personnel files, and any data covered by
GDPR / CCPA / HIPAA. Requires strict need-to-know access, encryption
at rest and in transit, and an audit trail of all access.

2.4 RESTRICTED
A subset of Confidential reserved for the most sensitive information
(e.g., production secrets, board materials, M&A documents). Access
requires explicit CIO or CEO approval and is logged at the keystroke
level.

================================================================================
3. ACCESS CONTROL
================================================================================

3.1 LEAST PRIVILEGE
Access to systems and data is granted on a least-privilege basis.
Employees receive the minimum access required to perform their job
function.

3.2 MULTI-FACTOR AUTHENTICATION
Multi-factor authentication is mandatory for:
- All production system access
- All cloud admin consoles
- All VPN connections
- All email accounts

3.3 ACCESS REVIEWS
Managers review direct-report access quarterly. Terminated employee
access is revoked within 4 hours of termination, automated where
possible via the HRIS-to-IdP integration.

================================================================================
4. ENCRYPTION STANDARDS
================================================================================

4.1 DATA IN TRANSIT
TLS 1.2 or higher is required for all network communications. Plaintext
protocols (HTTP, FTP, Telnet) are prohibited on production networks.

4.2 DATA AT REST
- Production databases: AES-256 with customer-managed keys (CMK)
- Laptops: full-disk encryption mandatory (FileVault on macOS,
  BitLocker on Windows)
- Backups: AES-256 with keys stored separately from the data
- Removable media (USB drives) used for Confidential data must be
  hardware-encrypted (e.g., Apricorn or similar FIPS 140-2 Level 3
  devices)

================================================================================
5. INCIDENT RESPONSE
================================================================================

5.1 REPORTING
Any suspected security incident, including phishing, unauthorized
access, lost devices, or anomalous system behavior, must be reported
to security@acmecorp.example within 1 hour of discovery.

5.2 SEVERITY LEVELS
- SEV-1 (Critical): confirmed breach, data exfiltration, or
  production outage affecting >10% of users
- SEV-2 (High): attempted breach, malware on a single device
- SEV-3 (Medium): policy violation, misconfiguration
- SEV-4 (Low): informational, near-miss

5.3 TIMELINES
- SEV-1: 15-minute initial response, hourly status updates
- SEV-2: 1-hour initial response, daily status updates
- SEV-3/4: next business day

5.4 BREACH NOTIFICATION
Confirmed breaches involving personal data trigger GDPR Article 33
notification to the supervisory authority within 72 hours. Affected
individuals are notified without undue delay where there is a high
risk to their rights.

================================================================================
6. VENDOR AND THIRD-PARTY MANAGEMENT
================================================================================

All third parties that process Confidential or Restricted data on
behalf of ACME must:
- Sign a Data Processing Agreement (DPA)
- Complete a security questionnaire annually
- Provide evidence of SOC 2 Type II or ISO 27001 certification
- Allow ACME to audit their controls on request

================================================================================
7. ACCEPTABLE USE
================================================================================

7.1 PERSONAL USE
Incidental personal use of company systems is permitted but must not
interfere with work, consume excessive resources, or violate any
other policy.

7.2 PROHIBITED ACTIVITIES
- Storing personal Confidential data on company systems
- Sharing account credentials
- Installing unapproved software on production systems
- Connecting unauthorized storage devices to production systems
- Bypassing security controls (e.g., disabling endpoint protection)

7.3 MONITORING
ACME reserves the right to monitor activity on company systems.
Employees should have no expectation of privacy when using company
equipment or networks.

================================================================================
8. TRAINING AND AWARENESS
================================================================================

All employees complete annual security awareness training. New
hires complete it within 14 days of start date. Failure to complete
training within 30 days of assignment results in account suspension
until training is complete.
