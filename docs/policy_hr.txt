SECTION 1: WORK HOURS AND FLEXIBILITY
================================================================================

1.1 STANDARD WORK WEEK
All full-time employees are expected to work a standard 40-hour work week,
typically scheduled as 8 hours per day, Monday through Friday.

1.2 FLEXIBLE WORK ARRANGEMENTS
Eligible employees may request flexible work arrangements including:
- Remote work (up to 3 days per week with manager approval)
- Compressed work weeks (e.g., four 10-hour days)
- Flexible start and end times within core hours of 10:00 AM to 3:00 PM
Requests must be submitted through the HR portal at least 14 days in advance.

1.3 OVERTIME
Non-exempt employees who work more than 40 hours in a work week will receive
overtime pay at 1.5x their regular hourly rate. All overtime must be
pre-approved by the direct manager.

================================================================================
SECTION 2: LEAVE POLICIES
================================================================================

2.1 PAID TIME OFF (PTO)
Full-time employees accrue 20 days of PTO per calendar year, accrued at
1.67 days per month. Unused PTO carries over up to a maximum of 10 days
into the next calendar year.

2.2 SICK LEAVE
Employees receive 10 days of paid sick leave per year, available from day one.
Sick leave does not carry over to the next calendar year.

2.3 PARENTAL LEAVE
Primary caregivers are entitled to 16 weeks of paid parental leave.
Secondary caregivers are entitled to 8 weeks of paid parental leave.
Parental leave must be taken within the first 12 months of the child's birth
or adoption.

2.4 BEREAVEMENT LEAVE
Employees may take up to 5 paid days off for the death of an immediate
family member, and up to 2 days for extended family.

================================================================================
SECTION 3: CODE OF CONDUCT
================================================================================

3.1 PROFESSIONAL BEHAVIOR
All employees are expected to conduct themselves professionally and treat
colleagues, clients, and partners with respect. Harassment, discrimination,
and retaliation of any kind are strictly prohibited and grounds for
immediate termination.

3.2 CONFLICTS OF INTEREST
Employees must disclose any actual or potential conflicts of interest to
their manager and the Compliance team. Examples include outside employment,
financial interests in vendors, and personal relationships within reporting
lines.

3.3 REPORTING VIOLATIONS
Suspected violations of this policy should be reported to HR, to the
Ethics Hotline at 1-800-555-0199, or anonymously through the online
EthicsPoint portal at https://ethics.acmecorp.example.

================================================================================
SECTION 4: COMPENSATION REVIEW
================================================================================

4.1 ANNUAL REVIEW CYCLE
Compensation reviews occur each March. Employees receive written feedback
on their performance and any adjustments to base pay, bonus, or equity.

4.2 PROMOTIONS
Promotions are considered during the annual review cycle and at other
times when business needs require. A promotion typically includes a
minimum 8% increase to base pay.

================================================================================
SECTION 5: TERMINATION
================================================================================

5.1 VOLUNTARY TERMINATION
Employees who choose to resign are asked to provide at least 2 weeks of
written notice. Final paychecks are issued on the next regular pay date.

5.2 INVOLUNTARY TERMINATION
Termination for cause (gross misconduct, theft, fraud) results in
immediate separation without notice. Termination for performance or
redundancy follows a documented performance management process with
at least 30 days notice where possible.

5.3 EXIT INTERVIEW
All departing employees are asked to participate in a confidential exit
interview with HR.
